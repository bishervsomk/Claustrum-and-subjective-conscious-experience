The Evidence Trail

The hypothesis is supported by multiple lines of evidence, from anatomical connections to sophisticated functional studies. The claustrum is not just a simple relay; it's a dynamic processor involved in higher-order cognitive functions.

Unparalleled Connectivity

The claustrum is the most densely connected structure in the brain for its size, with reciprocal connections to nearly all cortical areas. This makes it a prime candidate for integrating widespread information.

Salience & Attention

Functional studies show the claustrum is crucial for detecting important events and filtering out distractions, suggesting a role in attention and cognitive control rather than just simple sensory binding.

Synchronizing the Brain

The claustrum can act as a "conductor," coordinating slow-wave brain activity across the cortex. This provides a mechanism for binding distributed information into a single, coherent experience.

Situating the Claustrum in Theories of Consciousness

The idea of an 'internal screen' fits neatly within modern neuroscientific theories of consciousness. It suggests a two-stage process where the claustrum first creates a subjective experience and then gates what becomes objectively reportable.

1. The Subjective Experience (Integrated Information Theory - IIT)

IIT suggests consciousness is integrated information (Φ). The claustrum's immense connectivity makes it a prime candidate for a high-Φ structure, generating the rich, private, "what-it's-like" quality of experience—the 'internal screen' itself.

2. The Objective Report (Global Neuronal Workspace - GNW)

GNW theory posits that information becomes conscious when it's "broadcast" to a global workspace for report and control. The claustrum may act as a gatekeeper, evaluating the 'internal screen' and deciding which salient information gets broadcast, making it objectively reportable.

Conclusion: A New Model for an Old Enigma

The hypothesis presents a compelling, evidence-based model of claustrum function. It moves beyond simple integration, proposing that the claustrum constructs a meaningful, subjective world-model to guide purposeful, goal-directed behavior. This framework synthesizes anatomical, functional, and theoretical data into a coherent narrative.

Future Directions

The strength of this model lies in its testable predictions. Future research using optogenetics and multi-site recording can directly probe the claustro-putaminal pathway to confirm its role in flexible, goal-directed action, further illuminating the link between our internal world and our external behavior.
